Sunset Calculator

Source

NOAA's Solar Calculator (gml.noaa.gov/grad/solcalc) is used as a behavioral reference. The underlying ephemeris formulas follow Jean Meeus, Astronomical Algorithms (2nd ed., 1998), implemented via the SolarCalculator library (github.com/jpb10/SolarCalculator). This firmware path is tuned for single-precision floating-point arithmetic on embedded hardware.

The reported sunset is the instant the solar upper limb crosses the apparent horizon, using the standard atmospheric refraction assumption baked into the model. Civil, nautical, and astronomical twilight are out of scope for this calculation.

Accuracy & limitations

  • Meeus characterizes sunrise/sunset accuracy at approximately minute-level resolution.
  • Refraction is parameterized as a fixed standard value; real conditions vary with pressure, temperature, and observer altitude.
  • The geometric horizon is assumed at sea level, with no topographic or line-of-sight obstruction corrections (for example, terrain or buildings).
